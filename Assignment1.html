ZhangJiaJie National Forest Park


ZhangJiaJie is located in central China, northwest Hunan Province, over 1000 kilometers from both Shanghai and Beijing.
ZhangJiaJie is famous for its precarious peaks, limpid streams, dense forests, and large karst caves. Notably, the movie
"Avatar" was filmed mostly in ZhangJiaJie, which gained popularity after the blockbuster premiered in 2009.

Attractions


ZhangJiaJie Glass Bridge



Visitors cross the ZhangJiaJie Glass Bridge suspended 260 meters above a valley in ZhangJiaJie in China's Hunan
Province on August 21, 2016. It was the world's highest and longest glass-bottomed bridge when it opened.



Wulingyuan Scenic Area

Quartz sandstone pillars rise above the forested valley floor. The Bailong Elevator, a glass-enclosed observation
elevator that travels 326 meters up a cliffside in Wulingyuan Scenic Area, is one of the top sites to oversee
some of the most amazing views.



Tianmen Mountain


Tianmen Mountain, a famous mountain with a large naturally occuring cave called "Heaven's Gate" near its peak.
There are many exciting trails to hike that line the cliff walls of Tianmen Mountain.


My Experience

ZhangJiaJie is a must go for those who are nature lovers and also those who just love a good view. The highlight
for me was the glass-bottomed bridge that suspends incredibly high, which was pretty nerve-racking for me. If
you have a couple of extra days, you can opt to take a boat ride on Baofeng Lake and explore China's largest karst
cave, the Yellow Dragon Cave. Nearby Fenghuang Ancient Town can be a great option if you are interested in
Chinese ancient towns. This could be a good getaway from the hectic and fast-paced Singaporean life!
